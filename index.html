The Legend of Zelda: A Journey Through Time

The Legend of Zelda is one of Nintendo’s most beloved and long-running franchises. Since its debut in 1986, the series has captivated players with its mix of exploration, puzzle-solving, and action-packed adventure. From the original Legend of Zelda on the NES to the critically acclaimed Breath of the Wild and beyond, the franchise continues to inspire gamers worldwide. © 2025 Nintendo Fan Page

Key Zelda Games Over the Years

Below is a quick look at some major milestones in the series. Each entry brought new mechanics and worlds for players to explore:

Year Game Title Notable Feature
1986 The Legend of Zelda Introduced open-world exploration on the NES
1998 Ocarina of Time First 3D Zelda game, often hailed as one of the best games ever
2006 Twilight Princess Darker tone, introduced wolf transformation mechanic
2017 Breath of the Wild Massive open-world design → freedom like never before
2023 Tears of the Kingdom Expanded gameplay with sky islands and new abilities like Ultrahand

A Hero’s Journey

The hero of the series, Link is the main protagonist of The Legend of Zelda series and one of Nintendo's flagship characters. A young but powerful, highly adventurous swordsman dressed in green whose main objective is to protect the world from the forces of evil—generally the Kingdom of Hyrule—through incredible feats of courage and bravery as well as overcoming dangerous obstacles and hardships, Link made his grand debut in the titular first game, The Legend of Zelda. He has been present in every subsequent title since (with the sole exception being the Japan-only Satellaview games, which are non-canonical). He is one of the three most important characters in the franchise, alongside Princess Zelda and Ganon, with the two in particular being his close ally and archenemy, respectively.
